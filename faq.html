Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Luminaries of Islam

What is the Islamic Golden Age?
The Islamic Golden Age (roughly 8th–14th century CE) was a period of extraordinary cultural, economic, and scientific flourishing in the Islamic world. Scholars made groundbreaking advances in mathematics, medicine, astronomy, chemistry, engineering, and many other fields. Many of these contributions laid the groundwork for modern science.
Why are these scientists important?
These scholars made discoveries and inventions that shaped the modern world. From algebra and algorithms to optics, surgery, and the scientific method — their work is the foundation of much of what we use and know today. Learning about them connects us to a rich history of human curiosity and achievement.
Can I use this app offline?
Almost entirely! The app runs locally in your browser. The only external dependency is Google Fonts for typography. If you're offline, the app will work but may use fallback fonts. No data is ever sent to any server.
Does the app record my voice?
Absolutely not. The Speech-to-Text feature uses your browser's built-in recognition engine. No voice data is stored, recorded, or sent anywhere. Your privacy is fully protected.
Why is the microphone button not showing?
Voice recognition (Speech-to-Text) requires browser support. It works best in Google Chrome and Microsoft Edge. If your browser doesn't support it, the microphone button is automatically hidden, but all other features work perfectly via clicking and typing.
Can the app read to me?
Yes! Look for the 🔊 speaker icon throughout the app. Click it to have the app read scientist bios, fun facts, quiz questions, and more. You can control the speed with the control bar that appears at the bottom of the screen.
What can I say with voice search?
You can say any scientist's name (e.g., "Al-Khwarizmi", "Ibn Sina", "Al-Jazari") and the app will search for them. In the quiz, you can say an answer option out loud to select it.
How do I change the reading speed?
When TTS is playing, a control bar appears at the bottom. Use the speed buttons: 🐢 Slow, 🚶 Normal, or 🐇 Fast. Your preference is saved for next time.
Why Bismillah at the top?
"Bismillah" (In the Name of God, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful) is a traditional Islamic invocation said before undertaking any important task, including the pursuit of knowledge. It reflects the app's respect for Islamic values and the scholars' own faith-driven pursuit of understanding.
Is this app free?
Yes, completely free. It's an educational resource designed to share the inspiring stories of Islamic Golden Age scientists with learners of all ages.
How do I add more scientists?
If you're a developer, you can add scientists by editing the `scientists` array in `js/app.js`. Follow the same data structure as existing entries. Don't forget to add corresponding quiz questions too!
Does it read in Arabic?
Currently, the TTS reads in English. Arabic text-to-speech is a stretch goal for a future version. Arabic names of scientists are displayed in the app for reference.
